# OpenLib Project Context

OpenLib is an Education-area civic service for the Pi.Ca.Ci.U final presentation. It turns neighborhood libraries into a free distributed campus where citizens can find or offer peer-to-peer learning experiences.

## Language

**OpenLib**:
The service that connects people who want to learn practical skills with people in their neighborhood who can teach them inside local libraries.
_Avoid_: generic platform, online course marketplace

**Distributed campus**:
A network of neighborhood libraries used as accessible learning spaces rather than a single centralized school.
_Avoid_: school, academy, university

**Peer-to-peer learning**:
Learning organized between citizens with complementary skills, where people can alternate between learner and teacher roles.
_Avoid_: formal course, traditional lesson, private tutoring marketplace

**Learner**:
A citizen who uses OpenLib to find free practical learning opportunities in a nearby library.
_Avoid_: customer, student, buyer

**Peer-teacher**:
A citizen who uses OpenLib to share a practical skill with others in a nearby library.
_Avoid_: instructor, professor, seller

**Library partner**:
A local library that hosts OpenLib activities and provides the trusted physical setting for peer-to-peer learning.
_Avoid_: venue, classroom rental, generic location

**Light verification**:
The trust mechanism where a course proposal is checked by OpenLib and the library partner before being published.
_Avoid_: rigid accreditation, university certification

**Course activity**:
A free scheduled peer-to-peer learning session hosted in a library partner space.
_Avoid_: paid class, academic course, online lesson

**Course proposal**:
A peer-teacher submission that describes the skill, preferred library, availability, and contact details before light verification.
_Avoid_: listing, advertisement, commercial offer

## Relationships

- **OpenLib** uses the **distributed campus** model to make **peer-to-peer learning** local, free, and community-based.
- **OpenLib** gives equal narrative weight to **Learners** and **Peer-teachers** in the landing page.
- A **Peer-teacher** proposes an activity, then **OpenLib** and a **Library partner** apply **light verification** before the activity appears publicly.
- A **Learner** books a place in a **Course activity**; a **Peer-teacher** submits a **Course proposal**.

## Example Dialogue

> **Dev:** "Should the hero describe OpenLib as an online education platform?"
> **Domain expert:** "No. OpenLib is a distributed campus based in libraries; the digital touchpoint helps people find or offer peer-to-peer learning, but the value is the local human encounter."

> **Dev:** "Should the page optimize only for people looking for courses?"
> **Domain expert:** "No. OpenLib is two-sided: the page must make both finding a skill and sharing one feel equally central."

> **Dev:** "Can anyone publish a course instantly?"
> **Domain expert:** "No. OpenLib stays open and free, but each course passes through light verification with the library partner, plus community rules and feedback after the meeting."
